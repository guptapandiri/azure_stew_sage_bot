# StewSage — The Pitch Script

---

## [COLD OPEN — 3 seconds of silence]

**[Voice, low and deliberate]**

> *"How many tabs do you have open right now?"*

---

## [ACT I — THE PROBLEM]

**[Upbeat, relatable energy]**

Picture a Monday morning. Your standup starts in 8 minutes.

You've got Azure DevOps open in one tab. Teams in another. Your IDE in a third. GitHub — wait, no, ADO — for the PRs. And somewhere buried in there is the Slack thread where someone asked *"hey did that pipeline fail last night?"*

You're a developer. A brilliant one. But half your day isn't development — **it's context switching.**

You switch apps to check what's assigned to you. You switch again to update a status. You switch *again* to trigger a build. You switch *again* to look at why it failed. And somewhere in all that switching... the actual code never got written.

**We built StewSage to end that.**

---

## [ACT II — THE REVEAL]

**[Confident, product demo energy]**

StewSage is an AI-powered developer assistant that lives right inside **Microsoft Teams** — where you already are — and gives you complete command over your Azure DevOps workspace through plain English conversation.

No new tools. No new logins. No new dashboards.

**Just. Chat.**

---

## [ACT III — THE DEMO NARRATIVE]

**[Storytelling mode — walk through a developer's actual day]**

---

### 8:52 AM — Standup in 8 minutes

You type: *"show my bugs"*

Instantly — a beautiful card appears. Every open bug assigned to you. Titles, states, assignees. No clicking into ADO. No loading spinners. **Done in 3 seconds.**

---

### 9:15 AM — You pick up a ticket

You type: *"fix bug 3"*

And here's where StewSage does something **no other tool does.**

It doesn't ask you which file. It doesn't ask you what the problem is. It reads the bug description, **scans your entire repository**, and uses AI to identify exactly which files are relevant. It fetches those files. It reads the code. It generates a complete, working fix. It creates a new branch. It commits the changes. And it raises a Pull Request — linked directly to the bug — and **posts the PR link back to your chat.**

*You typed four words. StewSage wrote the code and opened the PR.*

---

### 11:30 AM — You need to raise a PR manually

You type: *"raise a PR"*

StewSage walks you through it — repo, source branch, target branch — all through interactive cards, right in Teams. No switching tabs. And when you hit create? It compares the branches, reads the commits, and **writes your PR title and description for you.** Professional. Clear. Done.

---

### 2:00 PM — The build breaks

You didn't even have to check. StewSage **notified you** the moment it happened.

*"❌ Pipeline Failed — Build #142 — branch: main — triggered by: Sarah"*

Proactive. Instant. In the chat.

You type: *"show build logs"*

StewSage pulls the failure logs from the failed pipeline tasks, surfaces the error, and then — **asks AI to diagnose it.** Root cause. Exact fix. Prevention. Three bullet points. Right there in the conversation.

---

### 3:45 PM — Quick housekeeping

*"assign item 5 to John"* — Done.
*"mark item 2 as resolved"* — Done.
*"add comment to item 3: blocked on design review"* — Done.

**Three work item updates. Three messages. Zero tab switches.**

---

### 4:50 PM — Someone asks a random question in the chat

*"hey what's the difference between useEffect and useLayoutEffect?"*

StewSage answers it. Fully. With code examples. Because it's not just a DevOps bot — it's a **senior developer on your team**, available 24/7.

---

**[Slow down, let it land]**

We didn't build StewSage to automate one thing. We built it to eliminate the friction between **your idea and your impact.**

Every minute a developer spends navigating tools instead of building features is a minute your product isn't moving forward. StewSage gives that time back.

**One conversation. Every part of your dev workflow.**

Work items. Code fixes. Pull requests. Pipelines. Build alerts. Team collaboration. Code answers.

**All of it. Right where your team already talks.**
